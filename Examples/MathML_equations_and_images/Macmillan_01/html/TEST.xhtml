  • 9-4 When methylamine is added to water, the following reversible reaction occurs. Answer the questions below about methylamine based on the reaction shown.

    In methylamine, C 1 is bonded to three H and a nitrogen, which is further bonded to two H. Nitrogen has a lone pair of electrons. In water, oxygen is bonded to two H and has two lone pairs of electrons. Methylammonium has a similar structure to that of methylamine, but nitrogen in methylammonium ion has three H and a nitrogen that carries a positive charge. In hydroxide ion, oxygen is single bonded to H and oxygen has three lone pairs of electrons. The oxygen carries a negative charge. Methylamine reacts with water to form a methylammonium ion and hydroxide ion. This is reaction is reversible.
